Before Contacting Support

Please verify the following first:

  • Aruba IAP cluster is running AOS 8 (versions 8.10-8.13) — check with show version
  • Your AP model is AP-3xx, AP-5xx, or AP-6xx series
  • REST API is enabled on your Virtual Controller (allow-rest-api + commit apply)
  • You can reach the VC IP from your iOS/macOS device on port 4343 (HTTPS)
  • Your credentials work via the VC web interface first

Frequently Asked Questions

What devices are supported?

iOS 16+ and macOS Tahoe 26.3.1 or later.

Does this work with HPE InstantOn?

No. NoCentral only works with Aruba IAP clusters running AOS 8 (versions 8.10-8.13). HPE InstantOn uses a completely different infrastructure and is not supported.

Does this work with Campus Controllers or Mobility Conductors?

Not yet. Current version supports Aruba Instant APs (IAP) only. Campus Controller support is potentially planned for a future release with no ETA.

Do I need an Aruba Central subscription?

No. NoCentral works directly with your Virtual Controller over your local network without any cloud dependencies.

How do I connect to my Virtual Controller?

Enter your VC's IP address, username, and password. NoCentral will securely store credentials in your system Keychain.

What does the amber warning triangle on an AP card mean?

An amber triangle indicates a constrained AP model — one that has a known firmware ceiling below the final AOS 8 release (8.13). The AP is not broken or offline; it simply cannot run firmware above its supported maximum train.

Tap the AP card to open its detail view. The Version Support section shows the exact minimum and maximum firmware versions for that model. If your cluster is running firmware within that range, the AP is fully operational. If you are planning a firmware upgrade, check the Upgrade Planner tab first.

What is the Upgrade Planner?

The Upgrade tab helps you understand the impact of a firmware upgrade on your AP fleet before you commit to it.

  • Current State — shows how many APs in your cluster have known firmware constraints
  • Firmware Upgrade Simulator — select a target firmware train (8.10, 8.11, 8.12, or 8.13) and see immediately which APs would remain supported and which would not
  • Remediation Options — for any AP that would be left behind, three options are outlined: replace it with a compatible model, isolate it on a separate cluster running a supported firmware train, or retire and remove it from the allowed-AP list

The compatibility data is bundled with the app and updated automatically in the background when a newer version is available.

What is the Offline Clients section in the Clients tab?

NoCentral keeps a record of wireless clients it has seen connected to your cluster. Clients that were previously online but are no longer present appear in an Offline section at the bottom of the Clients tab, showing when they were last seen, which AP they were connected to, and which SSID they used.

This is useful for tracking down devices that have left the network, investigating whether a specific device has ever connected, or identifying stale entries that are no longer relevant.

  • Client history is stored only on your device — it never leaves your device
  • The retention period is configurable in Settings → Client History (default 90 days)
  • Individual entries can be deleted by swiping left on iOS, or via the context menu on macOS
  • All offline clients for a site can be cleared at once from the section header menu

Common Issues

Can't connect to Virtual Controller

  • Verify REST API is enabled: allow-rest-api then commit apply
  • Check that VC IP is reachable on port 4343 (HTTPS)
  • Self-signed certificates are handled automatically — no manual trust needed
  • Ensure you're connecting to the master IAP (look for * next to IP in show aps)

Login fails with correct credentials

  • Test credentials via VC web UI first (https://VC-IP:4343)
  • Sessions expire after 15 minutes of inactivity
  • If you changed the password recently, delete and re-add the site in NoCentral

Missing APs or incomplete data

  • Connect to the VC IAP, not a member AP
  • During cluster elections, some data may be temporarily unavailable
  • Offline APs won't show real-time stats

App crashes or freezes

  • Force quit and restart the app
  • Verify your iOS/macOS version meets the minimum requirements
  • Very large clusters (100+ APs) may experience slower load times

Known Limitations

Triband APs: AP-6xx series with three radios currently only show radio0 and radio1 statistics. Radio2 support is not yet implemented because i dont have any AP-6xx hardware.
  • Large clusters: may load slowly, please be patient
  • Campus Controllers: Not supported (IAP only)
  • Mobility Conductors: Not supported (IAP only)
  • AP compatibility matrix covers most AP-3xx and AP-5xx models. Coverage for very new or discontinued models may be incomplete.
  • At the time of writing, AOS 8.13 is the final major version of AOS 8 — future development and compatibility depends on HPE.
